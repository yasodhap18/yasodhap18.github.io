Dr.A.P.J.Abdul Kalam

The missile man of India

Our humble tribute to an inspiring visionary

Here's a time line of Dr.A.P.J.Abdul Kalam's life:

  • 1931 - Born on October 15, in Rameswaram, Tamilnadu.
  • 1954 - Graduates in Physics from Saint Joseph's College,Tiruchirapalli.
  • 1960 - Gains degree in Aeronautical Engineering from Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1969 - Transferred to ISRO from the DRDO.
  • 1980 - India enters the space club by putting the Rohini satellite in the near earth orbit with the indegenous Satellite Launch Vehicle(SLV-III),developed under the stewardship f Dr.Kalam.
  • 1980-1990 - As a chief of the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme,he was responsible for the development and operationalisation of AGNI and PRITHVI Missiles.
  • 1981 - He received "Padma Bhushan" award.
  • 1990 - He received "Padma Vibhushan" award.
  • 1992-1999 - He works as a Chief Scientific Advisor to the PM and Secretary of the DRDO.
  • 1997 - He received "Bharat Ratna" award.
  • 1998 - India conducts the Pokhran II nuclear tests in May with Dr.Kalam as the chief project coordinator.
  • 1999-2001 - Principal Scientific Advisor to the Government of India.
  • 2002-2007 - President of India.
  • 2009 - He received the Hoover Medal from ASME Foundation,USA.
  • 2012 - He received Doctor of Laws(Honoris Causa) from the famous Simon Fraser University.
  • 2014 - He received Doctor of Science from Edinburgh University, UK.
  • 2015 - He was laid to rest at Rameswaram with full state honors.
